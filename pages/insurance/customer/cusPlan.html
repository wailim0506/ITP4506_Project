Third Party

What is Third-Party Insurance?

Third-party insurance covers damages to other vehicles and injuries to other people in the event of an accident where you are at fault. It is the minimum legal requirement for car insurance in many places.

Key Features:

  • Coverage for damages to third-party property.
  • Medical expenses for third-party injuries.
  • No coverage for your own vehicle damage.
  • Typically lower premiums compared to comprehensive plans.

Comprehensive

What is Comprehensive Insurance?

Comprehensive insurance provides broader coverage, including damages to your own vehicle as well as damages to third parties. It covers a variety of risks, including theft, vandalism, and natural disasters.

Key Features:

  • Covers damages to your own vehicle.
  • Includes third-party liability coverage.
  • Protection against theft and vandalism.
  • Higher premiums, but greater peace of mind.
